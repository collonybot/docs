The Activity page surfaces behavioral patterns across your community - message volume, active member counts, peak hours, and top contributors. Use it to understand when your community is most engaged and who's driving it.

## Summary stats

Five metrics for the selected time range:

| Metric | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Messages** | Total messages sent in this period |
| **Active members** | Unique members who sent at least one message |
| **Active last 7d** | Members active in a rolling 7-day window (regardless of selected range) |
| **Messages / member** | Average messages per member across all members |
| **Messages / active** | Average messages per active member only |

## Top user lists

Three ranked lists for the selected period:

- **Top Active Users** - highest message count
- **Top Reacted-on Users** - most emoji reactions received - a signal of community impact
- **Top Reacters** - members who react most to others' messages

## Activity by weekday

A bar chart showing message volume by day of the week. Identify which days your community is most active and align posting schedules or team coverage accordingly.

## Activity by hour (UTC)

Message volume broken down by hour of the day. Useful for scheduling announcements, setting bot active hours, and planning when human moderation is most needed.

## When is the community active? (weekday × hour)

A heatmap combining day and hour into a single grid. The darkest cells are your peak engagement windows - the ideal times for announcements, AMAs, or community events.

## Messages & emojis over time

A dual-line chart tracking message volume and emoji usage over the selected period. A spike in emoji activity without a corresponding message spike can indicate a viral message or community reaction to an external event.

## Daily active users

A chart of unique active members per day. Compare against message volume - if DAU is flat but messages per user are rising, a small group is dominating the conversation.

## Top emojis

The most-used emojis in your community for the selected period. A quick read on community mood that complements the Sentiment page.
