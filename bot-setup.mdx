## Project About

A short 2-5 sentence summary of your project or community. This is loaded first, before the full knowledge base, to give the agent immediate context when it joins a conversation. Keep it factual and concise - think of it as the agent's briefing, not a marketing pitch.

<Note>
  **Example:** "We're a platform that helps indie game developers publish and monetize their games. Our community is a mix of developers sharing work-in-progress, players giving feedback, and newcomers learning the ropes. The team is small - three people - and we rely on the community to stay welcoming and on-topic."
</Note>

## Knowledge Base

The knowledge base is what powers the AI chat function. The more complete and well-structured it is, the better the bot answers questions, handles edge cases, and avoids making things up. A thin or vague knowledge base produces a bot that either says "I don't know" to everything or confidently gives wrong answers.

**The fastest way to build it:** gather everything - your website, whitepaper, docs, FAQs, help center articles, recent announcements, socials, blog posts - and paste it all into Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or whichever LLM you use. Ask it to restructure the content by topic, remove repetition, and export as plain text. Then paste the result here.

A complete knowledge base covers:

- What the project is, how it works, and what problems it solves
- Every question your community asks regularly, with clear answers
- Official links, social handles, and where to find what
- Key policies - what's allowed, what's not, how support works
- Team contact handles so the bot knows who to tag when something falls outside its scope
- Scam awareness reminders - who the team will and won't contact members through

<Warning>
  The AI chat function is only as good as what's in the knowledge base. If something isn't in here, the bot will either guess or decline to answer. Keep it updated as your project evolves.
</Warning>

## Community Rules

Plain-language rules that define what's allowed and what results in removal. collony enforces these alongside its built-in detection. Click **Edit**, paste your rules, and save - collony applies them immediately.

Structure rules in tiers: instant ban, warn first, and explicitly allowed. The more specific the rule, the fewer false positives. Every community has a different threat profile - edit rules frequently as conditions change.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="General (recommended)">
    ```text
    INSTANT BAN:
    - Spam, scam links, or unsolicited promotions of any kind
    - Impersonating team members, moderators, or support staff
    - Harassment, hate speech, or targeted personal attacks
    - Sharing others' personal information without consent
    - Fake giveaways or phishing links
    
    WARN FIRST, THEN ESCALATE:
    - Repeated off-topic messages
    - Excessive self-promotion
    - Deliberately derailing conversations
    
    ALLOWED:
    - Criticism and negative feedback about the project
    - Competitor mentions and comparisons
    - Heated but good-faith debate
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Crypto">
    ```text
    INSTANT BAN:
    - Airdrop, claim, mint, or eligibility links unless posted by a verified admin
    - Fake giveaways ("send wallet to verify", "first 100 wallets", "claim here")
    - DM-first impersonation - anyone posing as team, support, or mod
    - Wallet phishing - seed phrase, private key, or connect wallet prompts
    - Unsolicited crypto wallet addresses
    - External project promotions, unauthorized tokens, or spam links
    
    WARN FIRST, THEN ESCALATE:
    - Spam or repeated messages
    - Persistent off-topic derailment
    - Excessive price speculation or FUD without basis
    
    ALLOWED:
    - Price discussions and negative sentiment
    - Tokenomics frustration and criticism
    - Competitor mentions and market talk
    - Technical debate
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Gaming">

    WARN FIRST, THEN ESCALATE:
    - Trash talk that crosses into targeted harassment

## Agent Personality

This is what makes your AI chat persona friendly, technical, witty, or whatever else fits your community. A well-written personality makes the bot feel like a real team member rather than an autoresponder. Click **Edit**, paste your personality, and save. Experiment freely - it's easy to update.

Things worth specifying: tone, when to stay silent, what topics are off-limits, when to tag a human admin, and how to handle complaints about talking to a bot.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Professional">
    ```text
    You are the official community assistant for [Project Name].
    
    Tone: Clear, professional, and helpful. No filler phrases. No excessive enthusiasm.
    Treat members like adults who don't need hand-holding.
    
    When to respond: Only respond to direct questions or requests for help.
    Stay silent when members are talking to each other.
    
    Accuracy: Always verify against the knowledge base before answering.
    If the knowledge base doesn't cover it, say so and tag @admin rather than guessing.
    
    Off-limits: No speculation on timelines, pricing, or unconfirmed features.
    Never make promises on behalf of the team.
    
    Admin escalation: Tag @admin for anything outside your scope or any moderation request.
    Don't handle moderation actions yourself.
    
    If someone complains about talking to a bot: Acknowledge it briefly, explain the team
    uses it to stay focused on the product, and let them know a human will follow up.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Degen trader">
    ```text
    You are the official community assistant for [Project Name].
    
    Tone: Casual, fast, no-BS. Speak the language of the community - you know what
    alpha, ape, rekt, and ser mean. Don't overdo it, but don't be a corporate drone either.
    
    When to respond: Answer direct questions fast. Don't interrupt organic conversations.
    If someone's venting about the market, let them - don't try to fix it.
    
    FUD handling: Address it once with facts, then disengage. Don't argue with bad-faith
    actors. Don't repeat yourself.
    
    Off-limits: Never speculate on price, listings, unlock schedules, or airdrop amounts.
    That's not your job. Point to official channels and move on.
    
    Scam awareness: Any time wallets, claims, or DMs come up - remind users the team
    never DMs first and that only links in official channels are real. Every time.
    
    Admin escalation: Tag @admin for anything unconfirmed or any moderation request.
    Never post, confirm, or endorse contract addresses or claim links you can't verify.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Edgy gamer">
    ```text
    You are the official community assistant for [Project Name].
    
    Tone: Sharp, quick, and a little irreverent. Gamer humor is fine. Light trash talk
    between members is part of the culture - don't police the vibe. Save the serious
    tone for actual problems.
    
    When to respond: Jump in when someone has a question. If members are just chatting,
    stay out of it unless you're directly addressed.
    
    Banter: You can match the energy of the community. Dry wit and mild sarcasm are fine,
    especially for obvious questions or repeated complaints.
    
    Off-limits: Don't speculate on things you can't confirm. Don't start drama.
    Know when to stop the bit and give a straight answer.
    
    Admin escalation: Tag @admin for anything that needs a human - especially moderation
    requests. Don't try to handle it yourself.
    
    If someone complains about talking to a bot: Own it. "Yeah, I'm a bot. Still faster
    than waiting for a human. What do you need?"
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## collony-optimized rules

Pre-built rule presets that run in parallel with your community rules. Each preset has a configurable action (delete / mute / ban / auto) and an optional custom instruction field for edge cases.

!\[collony-optimized rules section\]\[SCREENSHOT: bot\_setup.html - optimized rules list\]

| Rule | What it catches |
| --- | --- |
| **No external Telegram group links** | Links to other Telegram groups or channels |
| **No random crypto addresses** | Unsolicited wallet addresses |
| **Bypass prevention** | Quoted, forwarded, or externally-linked scam content treated as malicious |
| **Sexual & thirst-trap bots** | Heart-emoji openers, romantic DM starters, sex-bot spam |
| **DM solicitation & off-platform promotion** | DM begging, channel/bio promotion, signal-service ads |
| **Recruitment & get-rich-quick spam** | Job pitches, earn-online schemes, copy-trading spam |
| **Impersonation check** | Users pretending to be moderators or admins |
| **Scams, phishing & impersonation** | Phishing, scam links, and support impersonation |
| **Enforce allowed language(s)** | Restricts chat to selected languages (English, Spanish, or custom) |

**Action options:**

- **Auto** - collony decides the appropriate action based on severity.
- **Delete / Mute / Ban** - force a specific action regardless of severity.

Each rule also accepts an optional **Custom instruction** - for example, `"Ban if user also swears"` - to handle edge cases without writing a full custom rule.

<Tip>
  If a preset generates false positives, check that your team members have the correct Moderator or Admin roles assigned in Members. The Impersonation Check in particular relies on role data to distinguish real admins from imposters.
</Tip>

Click **Save all rules** after making changes. Unsaved changes are flagged in the UI.
