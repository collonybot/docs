Bot Setup is where you define what the agent knows and how it behaves. All changes are versioned - you can see who made the last edit and revert if needed.

## Project About

A short 2-5 sentence summary of your project or community. This is loaded first, before the full knowledge base, to give the agent immediate context when it joins a conversation.

Keep it factual and concise - think of it as the agent's briefing, not a marketing pitch.

<Note>
  **Example:** "We're a platform that helps indie game developers publish and monetize their games. Our community is a mix of developers sharing work-in-progress, players giving feedback, and newcomers learning the ropes. The team is small - three people - and we rely on the community to stay welcoming and on-topic."
</Note>

## Knowledge Base

The content the agent draws on when answering member questions. A thorough knowledge base is the single biggest factor in response quality - it's the difference between a bot that actually helps and one that makes things up.

**The fastest way to build it:** gather everything - your website, docs, FAQs, help articles, recent announcements, socials - and paste it all into Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or whichever LLM you use. Ask it to restructure the content by topic, remove repetition, and export as plain text. Then paste the result here.

Good knowledge bases cover: what the project is and how it works, common questions and their answers, official links, key policies, and your team's contact handles so the bot knows who to tag when a question falls outside what it can answer.

Click **Edit** to open the editor. Include your team's handles (e.g. `@alice, @bob`) so the bot can tag them when a question falls outside the knowledge base.

<Tip>
  The more specific your knowledge base, the fewer questions fall through to your team. Think about the 10 questions your community asks most often - make sure every one of them has a clear answer in here.
</Tip>

## Community Rules

Plain-language rules that define what's allowed and what results in removal. collony enforces these alongside its built-in detection.

Click **Edit**, paste your rules, and save. collony applies them immediately.

Structure your rules in two tiers: what gets an **instant ban** and what gets a **warning first**. Be explicit - the more specific the rule, the fewer false positives.

<Note>
  **Example structure:**

  **Instant removal:**

  - Spam, scam links, or unsolicited promotions
  - Impersonating team members, moderators, or support staff
  - Harassment, hate speech, or targeted personal attacks
  - Sharing others' personal information

  **Warn first, then escalate:**

  - Repeated off-topic messages
  - Excessive self-promotion
  - Deliberately derailing conversations

  **Allowed (don't over-moderate):**

  - Criticism and negative feedback
  - Competitor mentions and comparisons
  - Heated but good-faith debate

  Every community has a different threat profile. A gaming server deals with different problems than a SaaS support group or a creator community. Set rules to match your actual situation and don't be afraid to edit them as conditions change - the right ruleset takes a few iterations to dial in.
</Note>

## Agent Personality

This is what makes your AI chat persona friendly, technical, witty, or whatever else fits your community. A well-written personality makes the bot feel like a real team member rather than an autoresponder.

Click **Edit**, enter your personality guidelines, and save. Experiment - personality has a big impact on how members respond to the bot, and it's easy to update.

<Note>
  **Things worth specifying:**

  - Tone - casual and warm, technically precise, playful, or professionally neutral
  - When to stay silent - if two users are talking to each other, the bot shouldn't interrupt
  - What topics are off-limits - anything you don't want the bot speculating on
  - When to tag a human - moderation requests, escalations, anything the bot can't confidently answer
  - How to handle complaints about talking to a bot - don't be defensive, acknowledge it and explain briefly why the team uses one

  A concrete example: "Be friendly and clear. Use casual language but stay professional. Don't interrupt conversations between members - only respond when someone asks a direct question or needs help. For anything you're not sure about, tag @alice rather than guessing."
</Note>

## collony-optimized rules

Pre-built rule presets that run in parallel with your community rules. Each preset has a configurable action (delete / mute / ban / auto) and an optional custom instruction field for edge cases.

<Frame>
  ![Collony Rules](/images/collony-rules.png)
</Frame>

| Rule | What it catches |
| --- | --- |
| **No external Telegram group links** | Links to other Telegram groups or channels |
| **No random crypto addresses** | Unsolicited wallet addresses |
| **Bypass prevention** | Quoted, forwarded, or externally-linked scam content treated as malicious |
| **Sexual & thirst-trap bots** | Heart-emoji openers, romantic DM starters, sex-bot spam |
| **DM solicitation & off-platform promotion** | DM begging, channel/bio promotion, signal-service ads |
| **Recruitment & get-rich-quick spam** | Job pitches, earn-online schemes, copy-trading spam |
| **Impersonation check** | Users pretending to be moderators or admins |
| **Scams, phishing & impersonation** | Phishing, scam links, and support impersonation |
| **Enforce allowed language(s)** | Restricts chat to selected languages (English, Spanish, or custom) |

**Action options:**

- **Auto** - collony decides the appropriate action based on severity.
- **Delete / Mute / Ban** - force a specific action regardless of severity.

Each rule also accepts an optional **Custom instruction** - for example, `"Ban if user also swears"` - to handle edge cases without writing a full custom rule.

<Tip>
  If a preset generates false positives, check that your team members have the correct Moderator or Admin roles assigned in Members. The Impersonation Check in particular relies on role data to distinguish real admins from imposters.
</Tip>

Click **Save all rules** after making changes. Unsaved changes are flagged in the UI.
